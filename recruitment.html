Recruitment & Application Process

Join the BCIT Police Service

The BCIT Police Service is a fictional campus police service.

BCIT Police Patrol Car

BCITPS keeping the campus safe

Minimum Requirements

To “apply” to the fictional BCIT Police Service, applicants should meet the following basic requirements:

  • Be a current BCIT student.
  • Have an interest in public safety and helping others.
  • Be able to work as part of a team.
  • Have good communication and problem-solving skills.
  • Be willing to follow campus rules and policies.

Application Process

Below is a basic example of what an application process could look like. This is inspired by real police recruitment, but simplified for learning purposes.

Step 1: Learn About the Role

Read through this page and learn what the fictional BCIT Police Service does on campus. Think about why you want to join and how you could contribute to safety at BCIT.

Step 2: Check the Requirements

Make sure you meet the minimum requirements listed above. If there is anything you are unsure about, you would normally contact a recruiter for more information.

Step 3: Submit Your Application

In a real process, you would submit an online application form with your resume and a short cover letter. For this project, you can imagine filling out a form that asks about:

  • Your name and student number.
  • Your contact information.
  • Why you want to join the BCIT Police Service.
  • Any volunteer or leadership experience.

Step 4: Interview & Basic Screening

If this were real, applicants who meet the basic requirements might be invited to a short interview. The interview would focus on your behaviour, values, teamwork, and problem-solving skills.

Step 5: Training & Orientation

Successful applicants would attend a fictional training session at BCIT. Training might include:

  • Campus safety procedures.
  • How to communicate with students and staff.
  • Basic emergency response awareness.

Interested in Applying?

This recruitment page is for a school project only and is not a real job posting. However, if you are interested in campus safety or law enforcement in real life, you can visit official police department websites or talk to a BCIT advisor about related programs and careers.

For this project, you can pretend to “contact a recruiter” by visiting our Contact page and imagining what questions you would ask.
